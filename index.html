Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who is eligible to attend?

    All high school and college students are welcome. If you are younger and want to attend, email info@hshacks.com. You must register to participate. You must bring a valid student ID that matches your registration!

  • How much does it cost?

    The event is completely free for all high school and college students. Admission is free, food is free, and the schwag is free. Unfortunately, we will not be providing travel reimbursement.

  • What can I make?

    Anything! You can build for web, mobile, Pebble, Google Glass, wearables, etc. Just make sure the project is made during the hackathon!

  • What should I bring?

    A valid Student ID is required for entrance. Bring your laptop, phone, chargers, and any other electronics you’ll need for hacking. Also bring a sleeping bag, toiletries, and an open-mind! Keep your belongings with you at all times!

  • Do I need a team to sign up?

    Not at all! We will have plenty of time for you to make teams, if you don't have one. You can also use online Facebook groups and event pages to find people to work with. And there is nothing wrong with working alone!

  • What if I don’t know how to code?

    Not a problem, we will have industry professionals mentoring students throughout the event. We’ll be hosting workshops online and at the event to help everyone out. We highly recommend learning web development from codeacademy and treehouse and using online tutorials from there to learn.

  • Can my parents come?

    Unfortunately, we do not have enough space to accommodate parents during the day. Your parents can stay at home and rest this weekend, you will have everything you need there. More information for parents is available here!

  • Can I work on an existing project?

    Sorry, but for this hackathon, we ask that everything be brand new. All coding must be done at the event. However feel free to work on design, logos, or ideas, just no coding before the event.
